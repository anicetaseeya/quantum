Sources and methodology

Every number in this tool comes from a published dataset. This page documents exactly which indicator I used, where I sourced it, how I weighted it, and what it measures.

I built this tool to give Spanish quantum startups a structured, evidence-based way to think about international expansion. I wanted to avoid the usual approach of ranking countries based on reputation or intuition. Instead, I assigned each country a score derived entirely from published indicators — normalised to the same 0 to 100 scale, weighted by what matters most to each startup's situation, and adjusted for how easy it is to expand from Spain specifically. This page documents every data decision I made.

Spain compatibility bonus

The 15% Spain-fit adjustment

In addition to the 6 criteria above, I apply a fixed 15% weight to a Spain-fit score. This captures factors that are specifically relevant to a Spanish startup expanding abroad but that do not fit neatly into any single criterion.

EU single market membership
World Bank Ease of Doing Business 2023
Whether the target country is an EU member state. EU membership dramatically reduces setup friction — no subsidiary requirement, shared contract law, and free movement of people.
Weight within Spain-fit: 30%
English proficiency
EF English Proficiency Index 2024
Country-level English proficiency. Since I am scoring from the perspective of a Spanish team, high English proficiency in the target country reduces communication and hiring friction.
Weight within Spain-fit: 20%
Regulatory friction from Spain
World Bank Doing Business — Cross-border Trade 2023
How much regulatory friction a Spanish company faces when establishing a presence in the target country. Includes customs, legal requirements, and administrative burden.
Weight within Spain-fit: 20%
Geographic distance from Madrid
Calculated from geographic coordinates
Straight-line distance in kilometres from Madrid to the target country's main business hub. Shorter distance scores higher, reflecting lower travel cost and time zone alignment.
Weight within Spain-fit: 15%
Diversification value
Analytical estimate
How different the target market is from Spain in terms of economic structure and customer base. Markets very similar to Spain score lower here — they add less strategic diversification value.
Weight within Spain-fit: 15%
Data quality

Confidence tiers

Not all indicators carry the same certainty. I assigned each a confidence tier and discounted low-confidence indicators by 10% in the final calculation.

Tier Definition Examples Discount applied
High Published by a major international institution with transparent methodology and at least annual updates. World Bank WGI, EPO patent counts, KPMG tax tables, QS rankings, IMD Digital Competitiveness None — used at full value
Medium Based on survey data, proxy indicators, or datasets updated less frequently than every two years. INSEAD GTCI talent openness, Dealroom startup counts, WEF Executive Opinion Survey 5% discount applied
Low Analytical estimates where no clean published dataset exists. Based on triangulating multiple secondary sources. Pilot programme activity, research centre density for smaller markets, diversification value estimate 10% discount applied
Important limitation The confidence tier shown next to each country in the results reflects the weighted average data quality across all indicators used for that country. India and the Netherlands carry more medium and low-confidence indicators than Germany or Singapore, which is why their results come with a lower overall confidence rating. This does not mean those countries are worse choices — it means the scores are less certain and should be treated as directional rather than definitive.
